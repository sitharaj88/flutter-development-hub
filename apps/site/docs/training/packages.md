# Training Packages

This page turns the training offer into concrete, selectable program shapes. The package names and durations here are designed to feel clear and commercial while still being customizable.

## 1. Flutter Foundation Intensive

Best for:

- developers entering Flutter from web, Android, iOS, or general software backgrounds
- colleges and academies that want a practical starting track
- learners who need a structured first program rather than fragmented tutorials

Recommended format:

- 5 days intensive or 3 to 4 weekends
- 24 to 30 live hours
- beginner to intermediate level

What it covers:

- Dart essentials
- Flutter widgets, layouts, navigation, and theming
- responsive UI basics
- forms, validation, and reusable components
- introduction to state, APIs, and local storage

Expected outcome:

- participants can build complete app flows and understand how a Flutter project is put together

## 2. Corporate Enablement Sprint

Best for:

- engineering teams starting Flutter adoption
- companies that want internal capability without a long ramp-up
- product teams needing shared foundations quickly

Recommended format:

- 4 weeks
- 2 to 3 live sessions per week
- 20 to 24 live hours plus guided assignments

What it covers:

- Flutter foundations for team delivery
- architecture, folder structure, and state decisions
- API integration, authentication, and persistence
- testing, code review habits, and engineering standards

Expected outcome:

- teams move from experimentation to a more disciplined shared delivery model

## 3. Advanced Delivery Program

Best for:

- teams already building in Flutter but facing inconsistency, scaling problems, or quality issues
- organizations that need stronger architecture and release confidence

Recommended format:

- 6 weeks
- 24 to 36 live hours
- blended training, reviews, and implementation support

What it covers:

- maintainable application architecture
- scalable state management and feature organization
- release hygiene, testing, and performance
- engineering standards and reusable delivery patterns

Expected outcome:

- teams improve consistency, reduce rework, and build with stronger engineering judgment

## 4. Institutional Bootcamp

Best for:

- colleges, departments, and training centers
- student communities or placement-focused learning environments

Recommended format:

- 30 to 40 hours
- bootcamp or semester-friendly schedule
- hands-on labs and mini project checkpoints

What it covers:

- Flutter fundamentals through practical exercises
- UI building and application logic
- API-backed app development
- capstone project and presentation

Expected outcome:

- students produce stronger applied work and gain clearer exposure to professional Flutter workflows

## 5. Mentored Build Lab

Best for:

- startups and internal teams building a real product while learning
- founders who need training and delivery support together

Recommended format:

- 6 to 8 weeks
- mixed sessions for teaching, reviews, and product guidance
- implementation-led engagement

What it covers:

- selective curriculum mapped to the live product
- architecture and implementation decisions
- review checkpoints and delivery support
- documentation and next-step guidance

Expected outcome:

- the team learns in context while the product moves forward with less avoidable confusion

## Package customization notes

- every package can be customized for online, onsite, or hybrid delivery
- assessments, capstones, and starter repositories can be included based on the engagement
- corporate and institutional proposals can be shaped around audience size, maturity, and timeline
- the same package can be compressed, extended, or blended depending on the expected outcome
