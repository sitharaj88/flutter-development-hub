# Schedule and Duration

The same content can be delivered in different rhythms depending on the audience and business need.

## Recommended pacing models

| Format | Best for | Typical duration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Intensive | corporate bootcamps, workshops, short enablement bursts | 2 to 5 days |
| Cohort | students and mixed learner groups | 4 to 8 weeks |
| Corporate sprint | teams adopting Flutter | 3 to 6 weeks |
| Institutional bootcamp | colleges and training centers | 30 to 40 hours |
| Mentored build | startups and live product teams | 6 to 8 weeks |

## Sample weekly rhythm

- 2 live sessions for conceptual and applied learning
- 1 guided assignment or lab submission
- 1 review checkpoint or Q&A block
- optional office-hour style support for active cohorts or teams

## Session design recommendation

- 20 percent concept framing
- 50 percent guided implementation
- 20 percent hands-on exercise or extension
- 10 percent review, discussion, and next-step briefing

## Corporate scheduling guidance

- shorter, focused sessions usually work better than long lecture blocks
- engineering teams respond well to delivery-linked examples
- stakeholder time should be protected through outcome-driven agendas
- review checkpoints matter as much as the session content itself

## Institutional scheduling guidance

- repeatable lab structures help faculty and coordinators
- module-based progression works better than isolated talks
- capstone milestones improve retention and accountability
