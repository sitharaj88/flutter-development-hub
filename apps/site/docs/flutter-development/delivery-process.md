# Delivery Process

Serious clients want to understand how the work will move, not just what the service list says.

## Step 1: Discovery

- understand the product context, current codebase shape, and engineering goals
- identify where the main risks sit: architecture, quality, delivery speed, or team maturity
- decide whether the engagement is advisory, delivery-led, or blended with enablement

## Step 2: Scope framing

- define the outcome, timeline, key responsibilities, and working model
- agree on the level of direct implementation versus mentoring and review
- identify the artifacts that should remain after the engagement

## Step 3: Execution

- run architecture sessions, implementation work, reviews, and structured handoffs
- create momentum with decisions that improve both the product and the team
- keep documentation and engineering standards close to the actual work

## Step 4: Stabilization

- harden releases, clean up weak patterns, and close the highest-risk gaps
- translate learnings into reusable standards or team conventions
- leave the team stronger than it was before the engagement began

## Step 5: Continuity

- shift into periodic advisory, targeted mentoring, or retained support if needed
- expand into training or internal enablement when the organization is ready
- use the portal docs and apps as reusable assets for long-term growth
